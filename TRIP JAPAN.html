Japan Travel Situational Quiz (1-25)

Situation 1: Station & Transportation (Slide 1/5)
Q1. You are at the station and want to find the ticket gate. What do you ask a station staff member?
Hint: The word for "ticket gate" is "kaisatsu" (改札).
Answer: C. 改札はどこですか。(Kaisatsu wa doko desu ka.)
Q2. You want to ask how much the train fare to Kyoto is. What do you say?
Hint: "How much" is "ikura" (いくら) and "up to/until" is "made" (まで).
Answer: A. 京都までいくらですか。(Kyōto made ikura desu ka.)
Q3. A train arrives, but you are not sure if it goes to Shinjuku. What do you ask someone nearby?
Hint: You need to ask "Does this train go to...?" (Kono densha wa ... ni ikimasu ka.)
Answer: B. この電車は新宿に行きますか。(Kono densha wa Shinjuku ni ikimasu ka.)
Q4. You want to buy a Shinkansen ticket to Tokyo at the counter. What do you say?
Hint: "Ticket" is "Kippu" (切符) and "Please give me" is "Kudasai" (ください).
Answer: A. 東京までの切符をください。(Tōkyō made no kippu o kudasai.)
Q5. You want to store your heavy luggage before sightseeing. What do you look for?
Hint: It's a loan word (Katakana) for a place where you put coins to lock your bags.
Answer: B. コインロッカー (Koin rokkā)
Situation 2: At the Hotel (Slide 2/5)
Q6. You arrive at your hotel and approach the front desk. What do you say first?
Hint: Use the English loan word for registering at a hotel + "onegaishimasu".
Answer: B. チェックインをお願いします。(Chekkuin o onegaishimasu.)
Q7. You want to know if breakfast is included with your room. What do you ask?
Hint: "Is it included/attached?" is "tsuite imasu ka".
Answer: A. 朝食はついていますか。(Chōshoku wa tsuite imasu ka.)
Q8. Oh no! You lost your room key. What do you tell the receptionist?
Hint: "To lose" is "nakusu" (なくす). "...te shimaimashita" shows regret.
Answer: C. 部屋の鍵をなくしてしまいました。(Heya no kagi o nakushite shimaimashita.)
Q9. You arrived early. You want to ask if the hotel can keep your luggage before check-in.
Hint: "To keep/store something for someone" is "azukaru" (預かる).
Answer: B. 荷物を預かってもらえますか。(Nimotsu o azukatte moraemasu ka.)
Q10. You need a password to connect to the internet. What do you ask?
Hint: "What is it?" is "Nan desu ka".
Answer: A. Wi-Fiのパスワードは何ですか。(Wi-Fi no pasuwādo wa nan desu ka.)
Situation 3: At a Restaurant (Slide 3/5)
Q11. You enter a restaurant and the staff says "Nan-mei sama desu ka?" (How many people?). You are a group of 2.
Hint: The counter for people in formal situations (like restaurants) is "-mei" (名).
Answer: B. ２名です。(Ni-mei desu.)
Q12. You are ready to order your food. How do you get the waiter's attention?
Hint: Start with "Excuse me" (Sumimasen), followed by "Order, please" (Chūmon o onegaishimasu).
Answer: C. すみません、注文をお願いします。(Sumimasen, chūmon o onegaishimasu.)
Q13. You cannot read the Japanese menu. How do you ask if they have an English menu?
Hint: "Do you have...?" is "...wa arimasu ka".
Answer: A. 英語のメニューはありますか。(Eigo no menyū wa arimasu ka.)
Q14. You want to eat the restaurant's best dish. How do you ask "What do you recommend?"
Hint: The noun for "recommendation" is "Osusume" (おすすめ).
Answer: B. おすすめは何ですか。(Osusume wa nan desu ka.)
Q15. You are finished eating and want to pay the bill. What do you say?
Hint: "Check / bill" at a restaurant is "O-kaikei" (お会計).
Answer: A. お会計をお願いします。(O-kaikei o onegaishimasu.)
Situation 4: Shopping (Slide 4/5)
Q16. You see a beautiful fan. You want to ask the clerk "How much is this?"
Hint: "How much (money)" is "ikura".
Answer: A. これはいくらですか。(Kore wa ikura desu ka.)
Q17. You want to try on a t-shirt before buying it. How do you ask for permission?
Hint: "Trying on clothes" is "Shichaku" (試着). "May I...?" is "...te mo ii desu ka".
Answer: B. 試着してもいいですか。(Shichaku shite mo ii desu ka.)
Q18. You are buying souvenirs and want to know if you can pay by credit card.
Hint: "Can I use...?" is "Tsukaemasu ka" (使えますか).
Answer: A. クレジットカードは使えますか。(Kurejitto kādo wa tsukaemasu ka.)
Q19. You want to buy this item. What is the most natural way to say "I'll take this"?
Hint: Both "Kore ni shimasu" (I've decided on this) and "Kore o onegaishimasu" (This one, please) are very common.
Answer: D. A or C are both fine.
Q20. The cashier asks if you need a plastic shopping bag (Fukuro), but you have your own eco-bag. How do you decline politely?
Hint: "Kekkō desu" is a very standard and polite way to say "No thank you."
Answer: D. Both A and C are fine, but A is more polite.
Situation 5: Sightseeing & Asking Directions (Slide 5/5)
Q21. You are lost. You want to ask a passerby "Where is the station?"
Hint: "Where" is "doko". Start with "Sumimasen" to be polite!
Answer: A. 駅はどこですか。(Eki wa doko desu ka.)
Q22. You want to take a picture inside a museum, but you are not sure if it's allowed. What do you ask a staff member?
Hint: "To take (a picture)" is "toru" (撮る).
Answer: C. 写真を撮ってもいいですか。(Shashin o totte mo ii desu ka.)
Q23. You want someone to take a picture of you and your friends. How do you ask?
Hint: "...te moraemasen ka" means "Could you please (do something for me)?"
Answer: B. 写真を撮ってもらえませんか。(Shashin o totte moraemasen ka.)
Q24. You are at a beautiful shrine and want to know what time it closes.
Hint: "To close" is "shimaru" (閉まる). Also, D (Nan-ji made desu ka - Until what time is it?) is also perfectly acceptable and common!
Answer: C. 何時に閉まりますか。(Nan-ji ni shimarimasu ka.)
Q25. You are showing a map on your phone to someone. You want to ask "How do I get here?"
Hint: "How" is "dō" (どう). "I want to go" is "ikitai desu" (行きたいです).
Answer: D. Both A and C will be understood.
