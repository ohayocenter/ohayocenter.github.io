My Journey

A lifelong lover of travel, I spent my 20s and 30s exploring diverse cultures across Asia, North America, and Europe. The kindness I received from people worldwide inspired my deep fascination with the power of communication.

Now in my 40s, I balance my professional life as a stay-at-home father with my passion for teaching one-on-one Japanese lessons online at Ohayo Center. This role keeps me grounded, patient, and focused on helping others grow.

Like Fitness

Language learning is like weight training—something I've practiced for 20 years. You won't see results overnight, but consistency creates "muscle memory" that lasts a lifetime.

Why a "Cheat Day"?
Mastering a language requires a lot of self-study, which can sometimes feel exhausting. I want our lessons to be your "Cheat Day"—a refreshing space to rediscover the joy of Japanese and recharge your motivation so you never have to face the challenge alone.

My Teaching Philosophy

Listen – Speak – Read – Write
While I cover all four skills, my primary focus is on Speaking and Listening. I believe the best way to improve is to use the language as much as possible. I am here to ensure you can communicate naturally and confidently using "living Japanese."
