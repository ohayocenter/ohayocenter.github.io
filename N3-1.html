N3 Vocabulary Quiz (1-25)

Topic: Housework - Cooking (Slide 1/5)
Q1. When preparing a meal like 'Nikujaga,' what is the Japanese term for the action of 'peeling' (e.g., peeling the skin off a potato)?
Hint: Focus on the verb used specifically for removing the outer layer of fruits or vegetables.
Answer: D. 皮をむく (Kawa o muku)
Q2. What is the correct term for "boiling water" to make tea or soup?
Hint: The verb means "to bring to a boil," not just "to heat."
Answer: A. お湯を沸かす (O-yu o wakasu)
Q3. When making a salad, you need to "chop" the cabbage into small pieces. Which verb means "to chop/mince"?
Hint: Think of the rhythmic sound of cutting something finely on a cutting board.
Answer: B. 刻む (Kizamu)
Q4. Before eating, you might want to "season" or "flavor" the dish. What is the phrase for this?
Hint: The noun in this phrase literally means "taste" or "flavor".
Answer: A. 味をつける (Aji o tsukeru)
Q5. How do you say "to cook/boil rice" in Japanese?
Hint: This verb is almost exclusively used for preparing rice using a rice cooker or pot.
Answer: A. ご飯を炊く (Gohan o taku)
Topic: Housework - Cleaning (Slide 2/5)
Q6. What is the Japanese verb for "sweeping" the floor with a broom?
Hint: Think of the motion used with a broom (ほうき - hōki).
Answer: A. 床をはく (Yuka o haku)
Q7. After eating, you need to "wipe" the table. Which verb is used for wiping?
Hint: You use a wet towel (ふきん) to do this action.
Answer: B. ふく (Fuku)
Q8. How do you say "to vacuum" in Japanese?
Hint: This verb is often used with machines or devices, like an iron or engine.
Answer: B. 掃除機をかける (Sōjiki o kakeru)
Q9. When your room is messy, you need to "tidy it up". What is the phrase?
Hint: The kanji 片 means "one side" or "piece", implying putting pieces back in order.
Answer: C. 部屋を片付ける (Heya o katazukeru)
Q10. What is the action of "taking out the trash" to the collection point?
Hint: The focus is on moving it from inside the house to the outside collection point.
Answer: D. ゴミを出す (Gomi o dasu)
Topic: Housework - Laundry & Cleaning (Slide 3/5)
Q11. After washing clothes, you need to "hang them out to dry". What is the verb?
Hint: This word means exposing something to the sun or air to dry it.
Answer: A. 干す (Hosu)
Q12. Once the clothes are dry, you "bring them inside". What is the phrase?
Hint: A compound verb meaning to take (取る) something into (込む) the house.
Answer: A. 取り込む (Torikomu)
Q13. Before putting clothes in the drawer, you should "fold" them. What is the verb?
Hint: You do the same action to an umbrella or a map when putting it away.
Answer: B. たたむ (Tatamu)
Q14. What is the Japanese term for "dust" that accumulates on furniture?
Hint: You often "wipe" this off (〜をふく) or "sweep" it (〜を払う).
Answer: B. ほこり (Hokori)
Q15. How do you say "to iron" a shirt?
Hint: This is the exact same verb used with a vacuum cleaner (掃除機).
Answer: C. アイロンをかける (Airon o kakeru)
Topic: Housework & Childcare (Slide 4/5)
Q16. What is the Japanese word for "diaper", which you need to change for a baby?
Hint: The phrase is often used as "〇〇を換える (to change a ...)".
Answer: A. おむつ (Omutsu)
Q17. Which verb means "to pick up and carry a child in one's arms" (to hug/hold)?
Hint: A very common, affectionate word used when parents hold their babies in front of them.
Answer: B. だっこする (Dakkosuru)
Q18. What is the verb for "to carry a child on one's back" (piggyback ride)?
Hint: In contrast to holding a child in your arms, this means carrying them on your back.
Answer: C. おんぶする (Onbusuru)
Q19. If parents give a child everything they want and never scold them, they "spoil" the child. What is the Japanese verb for "to spoil"?
Hint: The kanji "甘" (sweet) is used to express being too lenient.
Answer: B. 甘やかす (Amayakasu)
Q20. How do you describe a child who is "selfish" or wants everything their own way?
Hint: The other options mean "quiet," "smart," and "admirable."
Answer: A. わがまま（な） (Wagamama)
Topic: Commuting & Trains (Slide 5/5)
Q21. What is the Japanese term for a very "crowded train," especially during morning rush hour?
Hint: "満員" (man'in) means "full capacity" or "packed with people."
Answer: C. 満員電車 (Man'in densha)
Q22. If you fall asleep on the train and miss your station, what verb do you use?
Hint: You pass (過ごす) the station you were supposed to get off at.
Answer: B. 乗り過ごす (Norisugosu)
Q23. When you see an elderly person standing, it is polite to "give up your seat." What is the phrase?
Hint: The verb means "to hand over" or "to yield."
Answer: D. 席をゆずる (Seki o yuzuru)
Q24. If a train is NOT crowded and you can easily find a seat, how do you describe it?
Hint: This is the opposite of 混んでいる (crowded).
Answer: B. すいている (Suite iru)
Q25. What is the verb for "changing trains" at a station?
Hint: "乗る" (ride) + "換える" (change).
Answer: D. 乗り換える (Norikaeru)
