Vol.3 🏠持ち家と賃貸：Owned House or Rental?

🎵 少しゆっくり・でも自然に話します😊

みなさん、こんにちは。
今日きょうのテーマは「持もち家いえと賃貸ちんたい」です。

みなさんは、持もち家いえと賃貸ちんたい、どっちがお得とくだと思おもいますか。これは日本にほんでもよく話題わだいになるテーマです。

持もち家いえのメリット：
・自分じぶんの家いえなので、自由じゆうにリフォームできます
・ローンを払はらい終おえたら、自分じぶんの財産ざいさんになります
・家族かぞくが安心あんしんして住すめます
・ペットも自由じゆうに飼かえます

賃貸ちんたいのメリット：
・引ひっ越こしが簡単かんたんです
・修理しゅうりはオーナーがしてくれます
・大おおきなローンを組くまなくていいです
・仕事しごとや生活せいかつに合あわせて、住すむ場所ばしょを変かえられます

今いま、日本にほんでは世界せかいのインフレとは反対に、デフレが続つづいています。そのため、不動産ふどうさんの価格かかくが安やすいと個人的こじんてきには思おもっています。私わたしの住すんでいる町まちでは、2000万まん円えんあればすごくきれいな家いえが買かえます。今日きょうのレートだと、約やく13万まんアメリカドルですね。古ふるい家いえであれば、ただ、つまり無料むりょうの家いえもあります。信じられないかもしれませんが、本当です。

最近さいきんは、DIYといって、無料むりょうの家いえを自分じぶんでリフォームする人ひとも多おおいです。「DIY」は「Do It Yourself」という意味で、「自分でやる」という意味です。私わたしもやってみたいのですが、日曜大工にちようだいくが苦手にがてなので、YouTubeで見みて楽たのしんでいます。

日本にほんでは少子高齢化しょうしこうれいかが進すすんでいます。将来は空き家が増えると言われています。もしかすると今後もっと価格は下がるかもしれません。

私わたしの夢ゆめは、いつか大おおきい家いえを買かうことです。庭にわがある家いえがいいなあと思っています。みなさんは、どんな家いえに住すみたいですか。

English Translation
Hello, everyone! Today’s theme is "Owning a Home vs. Renting." Which do you think is a better deal? This is a topic that is often discussed here in Japan. The Benefits of Owning a Home: Freedom to Renovate: Since it’s your own house, you can remodel it however you like. Asset Value: Once you finish paying off the mortgage, the house becomes your personal property. Stability: It provides a sense of security for your family. Pet-Friendly: You are free to keep pets without restrictions. The Benefits of Renting: Easy to Move: It’s very simple to relocate whenever you want. No Maintenance Stress: The landlord or owner handles all the repairs. No Large Debt: You don’t need to take out a massive long-term loan. Flexibility: You can change where you live to match your job or lifestyle changes. Currently, while the rest of the world faces inflation, Japan has continued to experience deflation. Because of this, I personally feel that real estate prices here are quite low. In the town where I live, you can buy a very beautiful house for 20 million yen. At today’s exchange rate, that’s about 130,000 U.S. dollars. There are even "free" houses—houses that cost zero yen—if they are old. It might be hard to believe, but it’s true! Recently, many people have been doing "DIY"—which stands for "Do It Yourself"—to renovate these free houses. I’d love to try it myself, but since I’m not very good at carpentry, I just enjoy watching others do it on YouTube. Japan’s population is aging, and the birthrate is declining. It’s predicted that the number of vacant houses will increase in the future. Because of this, prices might drop even further. My dream is to one day buy a big house. I’d love a place with a garden. How about you? What kind of house would you like to live in?

みなさん、こんにちは。今回は、日本人なら誰もが一度は悩む永遠のテーマ、「持ち家と賃貸」について語りたいと思います。

現在、日本では長年のデフレが続いており、世界的なインフレに比べて不動産価格が安いと感じられます。特に地方や過疎地域での「ゼロ円空き家」など、DIYリノベーションをして新しい住まいの形を模索する人も増えています。

English Translation
Hello, everyone. Today, I’d like to talk about a topic that almost every Japanese person has wrestled with at least once: the "eternal debate" of owning a home versus renting. This issue isn't just about financial gains or losses; it’s deeply connected to our lifestyles and personal values. From an asset-building perspective, owning a home offers a huge sense of security. Once the mortgage is paid off, you own a tangible physical asset, and the absence of rent payments in your later years provides a significant safety net. On the other hand, the greatest appeal of renting is flexibility. The freedom to move in response to job transfers or changes in family structure is perfectly suited to modern ways of working. However, with Japan’s declining birthrate and aging population, some forecasts suggest that the vacant house rate will exceed 30% by 2033. It might be necessary to look at real estate not just as a "place to live," but through a cool, analytical lens as an investment, carefully weighing the balance of supply and demand. How we choose and utilize the space we call "home"—where we spend the majority of our lives—is, in many ways, a choice about how we want to live our lives. If you’re interested, I encourage you to take a look at some Japanese real estate websites. You might just stumble upon a real hidden gem. Thank you for listening until the end. Goodbye for now!
