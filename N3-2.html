N3 Vocabulary-2 (1-25)

Topic: Holiday - Going Out (Slide 1/5)
Q1. What is the Japanese phrase for "making a plan" for a holiday or trip?
Hint: The verb means "to stand" or "to build/set up."
Answer: A. 予定を立てる (Yotei o tateru)
Q2. How do you say "to pack one's bags" or "prepare for a trip" in Japanese?
Hint: The verb implies bringing scattered things together into one place.
Answer: B. 荷物をまとめる (Nimotsu o matomeru)
Q3. What is the Japanese term for a "day trip" (a trip where you return on the same day)?
Hint: It literally translates to "day return."
Answer: A. 日帰り (Higaeri)
Q4. When you go on a trip, you need to "book" or "reserve" a hotel. What is the verb for "to reserve"?
Hint: This word is commonly used for hotels, restaurants, and tickets.
Answer: A. 予約する (Yoyaku suru)
Q5. How do you describe an "overseas trip"?
Hint: The first kanji (海) means "ocean" or "sea."
Answer: B. 海外旅行 (Kaigai ryokō)
Topic: Holiday - Relaxing at Home (Slide 2/5)
Q6. What is the phrase for "taking it easy" or "relaxing" at home on a day off?
Hint: It describes a slow, leisurely state without rushing or stressing.
Answer: A. のんびりする (Nonbiri suru)
Q7. How do you say "to sleep in" or "oversleep" on a Sunday morning?
Hint: This word combines "sleep" (寝) and "late" (坊).
Answer: C. 寝坊する (Nebō suru)
Q8. What is the term for "staying up late" to watch movies or play games?
Hint: The kanji "更" relates to growing late into the night.
Answer: A. 夜更かしする (Yofukashi suru)
Q9. If you are watching TV and want to "change the channel," what is the Japanese phrase?
Hint: The verb means "to change" or "alter."
Answer: A. チャンネルを変える (Channeru o kaeru)
Q10. What is the Japanese word for "a nap" during the day?
Hint: It literally translates to "noon sleep."
Answer: C. 昼寝 (Hirune)
Topic: Study - School & Tests (Slide 3/5)
Q11. What is the Japanese phrase for "attending a lecture" or "taking a class"?
Hint: The verb normally means "to exit," but in this context, it means "to attend."
Answer: A. 授業に出る (Jugyō ni deru)
Q12. How do you say "to pass an exam" in Japanese?
Hint: The kanji means "matching the standard."
Answer: B. 試験に合格する (Shiken ni gōkaku suru)
Q13. What is the action of "memorizing" vocabulary or kanji?
Hint: This implies learning something by heart.
Answer: C. 暗記する (Anki suru)
Q14. Before a big test, you need to "review" what you learned. What is the word for "review"?
Hint: The first kanji "復" means "to return" or "repeat."
Answer: B. 復習 (Fukushū)
Q15. If a student is absent from school without permission, they "skip class." What is the phrase?
Hint: The verb comes from the word "sabotage."
Answer: A. 授業をサボる (Jugyō o saboru)
Topic: Work - Office & Commuting (Slide 4/5)
Q16. What is the Japanese term for "going to work" (arriving at the office)?
Hint: The first kanji means "to exit/go out" and the second relates to "work/duty."
Answer: B. 出勤する (Shukkin suru)
Q17. How do you say "overtime work" when you have to stay late at the office?
Hint: It literally translates to "remaining business/work."
Answer: C. 残業 (Zangyō)
Q18. What is the phrase for "taking a day off" from work?
Hint: The verb means "to rest."
Answer: A. 会社を休む (Kaisha o yasumu)
Q19. When you travel to another city or country for business, what is it called?
Hint: It means a "business trip."
Answer: C. 出張 (Shutchō)
Q20. What is the term for a "meeting" with coworkers or clients in a formal setting?
Hint: It uses the kanji for "meet" and "discuss/consult."
Answer: B. 会議 (Kaigi)
Topic: Work - Business & Colleagues (Slide 5/5)
Q21. What is the Japanese word for your "colleague" or "coworker"?
Hint: It refers to someone at the same level as you in the workplace.
Answer: C. 同僚 (Dōryō)
Q22. How do you refer to your "boss" or "superior" at work?
Hint: The first kanji means "up" or "above."
Answer: A. 上司 (Jōshi)
Q23. When you swap business cards with someone, you are doing a "business card exchange." What is the word for a business card?
Hint: It contains the kanji for "name" (名).
Answer: C. 名刺 (Meishi)
Q24. What is the phrase used to say "Good work today" to a colleague leaving the office?
Hint: It comes from the verb meaning "to get tired."
Answer: B. お疲れ様でした (Otsukaresama deshita)
Q25. If you decide to "quit your job," what is the verb used?
Hint: The verb means "to stop" or "to resign."
Answer: B. 仕事を辞める (Shigoto o yameru)
